Flavor Pack — the 20% that makes it unforgettable

Nothing here is required to run the game. Everything here is what people will still be talking about next year. Steal what fits your crew's energy.

Opening skit — full staging (5 min of the 15)

1
Blackout + static.

Hall lights off. 10 seconds of loud static on the PA (any white-noise clip). Let it get uncomfortable. Then cut to dead silence — the silence is the jump-scare.

2
The discovery.

One flashlight beam finds the Intern (in costume) frozen mid-scream next to the taped body outline, holding a dropped coffee tray. Hold the tableau 3 seconds. Emcee's cold open starts over this image.

3
The suspect parade.

As the emcee names each suspect, that room master steps into a flashlight beam one at a time, does one signature gesture (Stella lowers her sunglasses; Reed checks his watch; Tan wipes his hands on his cloth; Echo scribbles; Joe strums one sour note; the janitor points at their throat), then walks off toward their room. Now every team knows every face.

4
Lights up, rules, dispatch.

Notebooks handed out DURING the rules brief so idle hands read the grid while listening. Teams dispatched by PA in radio-speak: "Unit One to the Live Studio. Unit Two to the Control Room…"

Fake sponsor ads — emcee filler between announcements

Read one every ~20 minutes in a golden-age announcer voice. They keep the PA warm, the world alive, and two of them are secretly clues.

Golden Mic Coffee

"This investigation is brought to you by GOLDEN MIC COFFEE. Rich. Dark. Unforgettable. Golden Mic Coffee — the last cup you'll ever need."

…secretly the method. Nobody notices until the reveal. Glorious.

Wraith & Sons Movers

"Heavy load? Awkward shape? Need it moved QUIETLY, at night, no questions asked? Wraith & Sons. We drag — pardon — we CARRY your burdens away."

…secretly the moved body. Same trick, second layer.

Dr. Static's Lozenges

"Lost your voice at the worst possible moment? Dr. Static's Throat Lozenges! Because some witnesses have so much to say… and no way to say it."

Nudge toward the Green Room for teams that haven't found the split clue.

1926 Time Signal

"At the tone, the time will be… LATER THAN YOU THINK. Beeeep. This has been the Radio 1926 time signal."

Use as the 1:45 time call. Cruel. Effective.

The station jingle

♪ "Nine-teen-twen-ty-six… the SIGNAL in the static, the TRUTH between the ticks… Raaaadio 1926!" ♪

Teach it to the crowd during the opening. Sing it before every PA announcement. By hour two the campers sing the response line themselves — congratulations, you've built a cult classic. (Jingle Joe should butcher it in his room, constantly, in the wrong key.)

Fun awards for the reveal (beyond top 3)

  • The Loudest Wrong — most confident failed accusation (they earned that PA announcement).
  • The Column A Award — best interrogation question a room master reports hearing.
  • Best Dressed Detective — self-explanatory; campers WILL dress up if you announce this beforehand.
  • The Static Award — the team that chased red herrings most magnificently. Presented with love.
  • Golden Snore — anything hilarious that happened at the Green Room. There will be something.

Photo moments (assign to floater)

  • Opening tableau (Intern + coffee tray) — shoot it at rehearsal too, in case the live one is dark.
  • Every team's first clue card — mugshot style against a wall, holding the card like an evidence board.
  • The moment after each WRONG announcement. Faces. Priceless.
  • Winning team recreates the crime scene with the actual solution for the closing slide.
Energy rule for all crew: commit to the bit. A committee that plays it straight-faced and slightly too seriously is 10× funnier than one that winks. The campers break character FOR you — never the other way around.
