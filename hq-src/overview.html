Dead Air — What this game is

In one sentence: 8 teams of campers race through 7 themed rooms, win a mini-game in each room to earn a clue card, and use those clues to be the first to correctly accuse WHO killed the DJ, HOW, and WHERE.

The story the players see

It's midnight at Radio 1926 FM. The station's star DJ — Maxwell "Mad Max" Loh, famous for filling the airwaves with gossip, hype and noise — is found dead in the Live Studio, headphones on, the transmitter broadcasting pure static. Six station staff were in the building. Every one of them had a reason. The teams are detectives called in before the police arrive.

How it plays, step by step

1
Opening (15 min).

Static blasts over the PA. Emcee narrates the discovery; helpers stage the crime scene. Each team receives a Detective Notebook — a Cluedo-style grid of all suspects, methods and locations, which they cross off as clues come in.

2
Investigation (2 hours).

Teams roam freely between 7 rooms. Each room = one costumed committee member (a suspect or witness) + one mini-game. One team in a room at a time, ~8–10 minutes per attempt.

3
Win the mini-game → get the clue card.

Each room pays out exactly one clue: it eliminates suspects, methods, or locations ("Stella was live on Channel 2 all night — innocent"). A team can only clear each room once.

4
Trade to complete the split clue.

The Green Room clue comes in two halves held by different teams — they must find each other and trade. This is deliberate: it forces inter-team interaction.

5
Accuse.

From the 45-minute mark, any team may go to the HQ desk and formally accuse (who + how + where). Wrong: 15-minute accusation lock-out, announced over the PA for drama. Right: they win. Game continues for 2nd/3rd. Reveal skit and debrief close the session.

Why it can't break

No team can fluke it

The full answer needs all 7 clues for certainty. Guessing early is possible but risky — the lock-out and public announcement make a wrong guess costly and funny.

No team can stall

The HQ desk sells a duplicate of any clue card for a 10-minute wait ("developing the film"). Slow teams always have a path forward.

No traffic jams

Teams start at staggered rooms (Team 1 → Room 1, Team 2 → Room 2 …). Room masters cap occupancy at one team and keep attempts under ~10 min.

Everyone plays

Mini-games require the whole team (relay, recording where all speak, charades). No game is solvable by one smart kid while seven watch.

The camp theme tie-in

"Unplug from the noise, sync with the Spirit." Mad Max filled the airwaves with noise; every suspect's motive is real, every red herring plausible — that's the noise. Teams win by tuning it out until only the true signal remains. The debrief makes this connection in two minutes, one verse, no sermon. The whole game is the theme.
