Crew · Voice of the game

Emcee — opening, PA drama, reveal, debrief

You are the radio announcer voice of the whole evening. Old-timey, theatrical, a little too fond of your own transitions. You carry the three set-pieces: the opening, the announcements, the reveal.

Opening script (0:00–0:15)

1

Cold open: loud static over the PA (10 s), cut to silence. Then, in announcer voice:

"Good evening, listeners. This is Radio 1926 FM… or it was. At the stroke of midnight, the voice that never stopped talking — finally stopped. DJ Maxwell 'Mad Max' Loh: found in his studio chair. Headphones on. Microphone live. Broadcasting… nothing. Dead. Air."
2

Setup: "Six members of staff were in the building. Every one of them had a reason. The police are forty minutes away. You — are not." Introduce the three questions: WHO. HOW. WHERE. Hold up the notebook, explain crossing off.

3

Rules: read the 7 player rules from the Run Sheet verbatim. Dispatch teams to staggered starting rooms. Start the clock. "The station is yours, detectives. Mind the static."

PA announcements during play

  • Failed accusation (radio from HQ): "Attention all units. Team [N] made a formal accusation… and was WRONG. The killer remains at large. Team [N] is benched from accusing for fifteen minutes. Carry on."
  • Time calls: at 1:00 ("halfway to the police arriving"), 1:45 ("fifteen minutes to the final window"), 2:15 ("FINAL WINDOW — every team to HQ").
  • Winner: dramatic sting moment — coordinate with HQ so the announcement lands right after the terminal's sting.

Reveal script (2:35–3:00)

1

Walk the giant grid clue by clue — cross off in the same order teams did: wounds (card 1), the dead mic and Stella's tape (card 2), the dust (card 3), the telegraph receipt (card 4), the sleeping jingle-man (card 5), the locked storage (card 6), the lawyer's slip (card 7). Land on: Tan. Coffee. Control Room.

2

Tan's confession (Tan performs, you narrate around it): the humiliation, the thermos, Max storming into the Control Room, the drag marks, the static. Keep it 60–90 seconds.

3

Winners: first correct accusation, then final-window accuracy placings (HQ hands you the terminal ranking). Prizes.

4

Debrief (2 min, land it and stop):

"Every suspect had a motive. Every rumor was plausible. The room was FULL of noise — and the answer was never in the noise. You solved this by tuning it out until one true signal remained. That's this whole camp in one game: unplug from the noise… sync with the Spirit."

One verse (suggestion: 1 Kings 19:11–12, the still small voice). Pray. Done.

Props you prepare

  • PA/mic access + a static sound (any white-noise clip on a phone into the mic works)
  • Giant elimination grid + marker for the reveal
  • Printed scripts (this page) — don't improvise the debrief, it's timed
