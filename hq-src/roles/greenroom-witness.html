Station 5 · Green Room

The Silent Witness — janitor with laryngitis

Your character

The station janitor. You saw useful things that night — but you have total laryngitis (doctor's note pinned to your shirt). You never speak, not once, the entire game. Everything is mime, gestures, and mouthing words. You are not a suspect — you're a witness.

Costume

Scarf around throat, doctor's note pinned on, mop or cleaning cloth.

Your room

Cozy corner: couch/chairs (Joe "slept" here), snack wrappers, a thermos on a side table (subtle coffee nudge — never point at it).

Your mini-game — "The Silent Witness" HANDMADE

1

Team enters; you point at the doctor's note, shrug apologetically. Your story must be extracted by charades / lip-reading / yes-no nodding.

2

The story you mime: (a) man with guitar (strum gesture) lay on couch at 11 (point at clock, then couch, mime snoring); (b) he never left (walk fingers, cross arms NO); (c) you also never left this room (point at self, at floor, shake head).

3

The team must repeat the full story back to you correctly (all 3 beats). Nod solemnly → hand Clue Card 5 — Half A plus one paper token. Mime: "other half… out there… another team" (point at token, point at door, hold up two fingers, interlock hands).

Why the split clue matters: Half B is only obtainable at the HQ desk by two token-holding teams meeting and presenting tokens together. You cannot explain this verbally — that's the fun. If a team truly doesn't get it after your mime, point at the token and mouth "H-Q" slowly.

What you know / never reveal

  • Everything above, in mime only.
  • NEVER speak. Not to answer rules questions, not when laughed at, not after your shift. Breaking silence breaks the room.
  • You saw nothing about the murder itself — if teams push (mime "did you see the killer?"), big sad shrug, shake head.
  • Never gesture toward the thermos. It sits there. That's all.
