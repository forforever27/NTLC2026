Crew · Detective's Office

HQ Desk — accusations, hints, tokens, scoreboard

Two people. You are the referees. Neutral, procedural, slightly noir — think "precinct desk sergeant". You are NOT suspects and know the full solution; your poker face is the most important costume in the game.

Your four jobs

1 · Accusation Terminal

Laptop runs /props/accuse/. On game day, open the GM setup screen (gear icon), set the answer (Tuner Tan / Poisoned coffee / Control Room) and the open-time (0:45). Teams select who/how/where and submit. The app enforces the 15-min lockout, logs every attempt, and plays the sting on a win. Radio the emcee after every attempt so it's announced.

2 · Hint sales

Any team may buy a duplicate of any room's clue card for a 10-minute wait. Take their team number, note the time, hand the card when time's up: "Your film has developed, detective." Keep a full spare set of all 7 clue cards.

3 · Split-clue tokens

You hold the Half B cards. When TWO teams each present a Green Room token together at your desk, both teams receive Half B. One team alone: "Regulations. Two witnesses to unseal testimony. Find another token holder."

4 · Scoreboard

Whiteboard: rooms cleared per team (room masters radio you or floater relays), accusations made (✗/✓), hints bought. Public info only — never the content of clues.

Procedures

  • Accusation flow: whole team present → confirm "this is a formal accusation" → they enter it in the terminal themselves → read result deadpan → radio emcee.
  • Final window (2:15): switch the terminal to Final Window mode (GM screen) — lockouts off, every team submits once. The terminal ranks by accuracy then time.
  • Disputes: you decide, emcee announces, no appeals. Rule of thumb: generosity on mini-game judging, zero flexibility on accusation rules.

Lines you may enjoy

"An accusation is a serious instrument, detective. The city remembers the careless."
"Wrong. The killer is still out there — and now they know you're guessing." (after a failed accusation)
"Two witnesses to unseal testimony. Regulations."

Never do

  • React to near-miss accusations ("ooh, so close") — deadpan only.
  • Reveal how many parts of a failed accusation were right. The terminal says only WRONG. That's the design.
  • Let teams hover near the terminal while another team accuses.

Props you prepare

  • Laptop with terminal loaded, GM answer set, tested (do a fake run the night before)
  • Whiteboard + markers, timer/phone, spare full clue-card set, Half B cards ×8, token match-list
  • Paper accusation slips (fallback if laptop dies) + the answer in a sealed envelope
