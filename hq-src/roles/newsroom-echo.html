Station 4 · Newsroom

Miss Echo — newsreader with a spiked story

Your character

Sharp, principled investigative newsreader. Max killed your career-making exposé to protect his advertisers. You despise what he stood for — noise over truth — but you're innocent: you were at the telegraph office filing the story anyway, 11:30 pm–12:45 am, receipt to prove it.

Costume

Notepad, pencil behind ear, crisp shirt.

Your room

Desk with typewriter vibes (paper stacks), the noticeboard fact sheet on the wall, Joe's crumpled royalty invoice in the bin (red herring — let them find it), and the shredded article strips in an envelope.

Your mini-game — "Stop the Presses" HANDMADE

1

Brief: "Someone shredded my draft. Thirty-six strips — but only thirty are mine. Rebuild it, and tell me which sentence is the FORGERY. A journalist checks her facts." Point at the noticeboard.

2

Team reassembles the article. Six fake strips don't fit the flow; ONE planted sentence reads naturally but contradicts a fact on the noticeboard (e.g. article says "the gala was on the 12th", noticeboard says the 14th).

3

Correct assembly + correct false sentence identified → hand Clue Card 4: "Facts checked. Here's mine." Stuck >10 min → hint: "Check every DATE against my board."
