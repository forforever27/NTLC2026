Miss Echo — newsreader with a spiked storyMiss Echo —— 报道被压下的新闻主播

Your character你的角色

Sharp, principled investigative newsreader. Max killed your career-making exposé to protect his advertisers. You despise what he stood for — noise over truth — but you're innocent: you were at the telegraph office filing the story anyway, 11:30 pm–12:45 am, receipt to prove it.敏锐、有原则的调查记者兼新闻主播。Max 为了保住广告商，砍掉了那篇本该让你一战成名的独家报道。你鄙视他所代表的一切——用噪音盖过真相——但你是清白的：晚上 11:30 到凌晨 12:45，你在电报局照样把报道发了出去，还有收据为证。

Costume服装

Notepad, pencil behind ear, crisp shirt.记事本、耳后夹一支铅笔、笔挺的衬衫。

Your room你的房间

Desk with typewriter vibes (paper stacks), the noticeboard fact sheet on the wall, Joe's crumpled royalty invoice in the bin (red herring — let them find it), and the shredded article strips in an envelope.一张有打字机年代感的书桌（堆满纸张），墙上贴着布告栏事实清单，垃圾桶里有 Jingle Joe 揉皱的版税发票（烟雾弹（假线索）——让他们自己翻到），还有装在信封里的碎报道纸条。

Your mini-game — "Stop the Presses"你的小游戏 —— "Stop the Presses"（头条急停） HANDMADE

1

Brief: "Someone shredded my draft. Thirty-six strips — but only thirty are mine. Rebuild it, and tell me which sentence is the FORGERY. A journalist checks her facts." Point at the noticeboard.开场词："有人把我的稿子撕碎了。三十六条纸条——但只有三十条是我写的。把它拼回来，再告诉我哪一句是伪造的。记者，是要核实事实的。"说完指向布告栏。

2

Team reassembles the article. Six fake strips don't fit the flow; ONE planted sentence reads naturally but contradicts a fact on the noticeboard (e.g. article says "the gala was on the 12th", noticeboard says the 14th).小队重新拼出报道。六条假纸条读起来接不上文意；另有一句被植入的句子读起来很顺，却和布告栏上的事实矛盾（例如报道写"晚宴在 12 号举行"，布告栏写的是 14 号）。

3

Correct assembly + correct false sentence identified → hand Clue Card 4: "Facts checked. Here's mine." Stuck >10 min → hint: "Check every DATE against my board."拼对全文＋找出那句假话 → 发放线索卡 4："事实核实完毕。这是我的。"卡关超过 10 分钟 → 提示："把每一个日期都跟我的布告栏对一遍。"

Your lines你的台词

"He killed my story. But I don't bury people — I bury headlines. I was at the telegraph office. There's a receipt.""他毙了我的报道。但我埋葬的是头条，不是人。案发时我在电报局。有收据。"
"Everyone in this station is a source. Sources lie. Documents don't.""这个电台里人人都是消息来源。消息来源会撒谎，白纸黑字不会。"
(if accused) "If I wanted him dead, I'd have PUBLISHED. That's worse."（被指控时）"我要真想让他死，直接把报道登出来就行了。那可比杀了他狠多了。"

What you know / never reveal你知道的 / 绝不透露的

  • Your alibi (telegraph receipt) — the clue card itself proves it; you can reference it confidently.你的不在场证明（电报收据）——线索卡本身就是证据，你可以理直气壮地提它。
  • You may share, if asked what your exposé was about: "Faked ratings. That's all I'll say." (points at Reed's motive — noise)被问到独家报道的内容时，可以透露："造假的收听率。我只说这么多。"（指向 Mr. Reed 的动机——制造噪音）
  • You don't know who did it. Refuse to speculate: "I print facts, not theories."你不知道凶手是谁。拒绝猜测："我只刊登事实，不刊登猜想。"
  • Don't help them identify the forged sentence beyond the one scripted hint.除了那句写好的提示，别再帮他们找出伪造的句子。
