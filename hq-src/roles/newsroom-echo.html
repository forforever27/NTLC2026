Miss Echo — newsreader whose big story was blockedMiss Echo —— 报道被压下的新闻主播

Your character你的角色

A sharp newsreader with strong principles who digs for the truth. You had a big story that would have made your career — Max blocked it to protect his advertisers. You hate everything he stood for — noise over truth — but you're innocent: you were at the telegraph office sending the story out anyway, 11:30 pm–12:45 am, with a receipt to prove it.反应快、有原则的调查记者兼新闻主播。你本来有一篇会让你出名的独家报道，Max 为了保住广告商把它压了下来。你看不起他那一套——用噪音盖住真相——但你是清白的：晚上 11:30 到凌晨 12:45，你人在电报局，照样把报道发了出去，还有收据可以证明。

Costume服装

Notepad, pencil behind ear, crisp shirt.记事本、耳后夹一支铅笔、笔挺的衬衫。

Your room你的房间

A desk with an old typewriter feel (stacks of paper), the noticeboard fact sheet on the wall, Joe's crumpled royalty invoice in the bin (a fake clue — let them find it), and the shredded article strips in an envelope.一张老式打字机风格的书桌（堆满纸），墙上贴布告栏事实清单，垃圾桶里放 Jingle Joe 揉皱的版税发票（假线索——让他们自己翻出来），还有装在信封里的碎报道纸条。

Your mini-game — "Stop the Presses"你的小游戏 —— "Stop the Presses"（头条急停） HANDMADE

1

Say: "Someone shredded my draft. Thirty-six strips — but only thirty are mine. Rebuild it, and tell me which sentence is the FAKE. A journalist checks her facts." Then point at the noticeboard.开场词："有人把我的稿撕碎了。三十六条纸条——但只有三十条是我写的。拼回去，然后告诉我哪一句是假的。做记者的，事实一定要查证。"讲完指向布告栏。

2

The team puts the article back together. Six fake strips don't fit the story at all. But ONE planted sentence reads smoothly — it just doesn't match a fact on the noticeboard (e.g. the article says "the gala was on the 12th", but the noticeboard says the 14th).小队把报道拼回来。六条假纸条读起来接不上；另外有一句是塞进去的，读起来很顺，但跟布告栏上的事实对不上（比如报道写"晚宴 12 号办"，布告栏写的是 14 号）。

3

When they rebuild the article AND find the false sentence, give them Clue Card 4 and say: "Facts checked. Here's mine." If they're stuck for more than 10 minutes, hint: "Check every DATE against my board."全文拼对＋找出那句假话 → 发线索卡 4："事实查完了。这个给你们。"卡超过 10 分钟 → 提示："把每一个日期拿去跟我的布告栏对一对。"

Your lines你的台词

"He buried my story. But I bury headlines, not people. I was at the telegraph office. There's a receipt.""他压死了我的报道。但我埋的是头条，不是人。那个时候我人在电报局。有收据。"
"Everyone in this station is a source. People lie. Paper doesn't.""这个电台里，每个人都是消息来源。人会骗你，白纸黑字不会。"
(if accused) "If I wanted him dead, I'd have PUBLISHED. That's worse."（有人说你是凶手时）"我要真想让他死，直接把报道登出来就够了。那比杀了他狠多了。"

What you know / never reveal你知道的 / 绝不透露的

  • Your alibi — your proof you were somewhere else — is the telegraph receipt. The clue card itself proves it, so you can talk about it with full confidence.你的不在场证明（电报收据）——线索卡本身就是证据，所以你提起来可以很有底气。
  • If someone asks what your big story was about, you may say: "Faked ratings. That's all I'll say." (this points at Mr. Reed's motive — it's just a distraction)有人问你的独家报道写什么，可以讲："造假的收听率。我只讲这么多。"（指向 Mr. Reed 的动机——制造噪音）
  • You don't know who did it. If they ask you to guess, refuse: "I print facts, not theories."你不知道凶手是谁。有人要你猜就拒绝："我只登事实，不登猜想。"
  • Don't help them find the fake sentence beyond the one hint written above.除了那句写好的提示，不要再帮他们找那句假话。
