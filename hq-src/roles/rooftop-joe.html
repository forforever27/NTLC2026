Station 6 · Rooftop Antenna

Jingle Joe — the man who speaks in jingles

Your character

Sweet, broke jingle writer. Max mocked your songs on air and the station never paid your royalties. You cope by turning everything into a jingle — literally everything you say comes out half-sung. You're innocent: you slept through the whole night on the Green Room couch.

Costume

Ukulele/guitar/kazoo (or air-guitar committedly). Loud shirt.

Your room

"Rooftop": antenna made of cardboard/broom + foil, string "cables", the storage inventory sheet posted (it's the basis of your clue). Needs a sight-gap for the relay — a corridor, two ends of a hall, or an outdoor stretch.

Your mini-game — "Signal Relay" HANDMADE optional app

1

Brief (sung, obviously): "♪ The antenna's dead, the phones are down, the only signal left in town… is LIGHT! ♪" Split the team: half are Senders (far end, with a word card + Morse chart + phone flashlight), half are Receivers (your end, Morse chart + pen). No talking or gesturing across the gap.

2

Senders transmit the word by flashlight blinks (short/long). Receivers decode and write it down. Word 1: STATIC. Then swap roles. Word 2: ANTENNA.

3

Both words correct → hand Clue Card 6. Optional: receivers verify via the Morse Checker app at /props/morse/, which shows completion code SIGNAL-6 they must tell you. Stuck >10 min → shorten word 2 to "SOS".

Your lines (half-sung)

"♪ I slept through the whole thing, on the couch of the Green Room ♪ — ask the janitor! Oh. Right. Laryngitis. ♪ Awkwaaaard ♪"
(if accused) "♪ You think that I'm the killer? That's a catchy little theory — but it won't chart. ♪"

What you know / never reveal

  • Your alibi: asleep in the Green Room all night (the witness can confirm — send teams there).
  • You know the storage inventory was signed at 9 pm — "all the heavy gear was locked away, I watched them do it" (supports your own clue).
  • You don't know who did it. Deflect musically.
  • Don't reveal what SIGNAL-6 or the words mean — they're just transmission tests.
