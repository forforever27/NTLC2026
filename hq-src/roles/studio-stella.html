Station 1 · Live Studio

"Static" Stella — rival DJ

Your character

Glamorous, salty rival DJ whose show was cancelled to give Max a double slot. You resent him openly — you're the obvious suspect and you know it, which annoys you. You are innocent: you were live on Channel 2 from 11 pm to 1 am.

Costume

Headphones around neck, scarf, sunglasses. Diva energy.

Your room

The crime scene: masking-tape body outline in the DJ chair area, caution tape, cracked vinyl record near the body (red herring — leave it obvious), ON AIR sign, cardboard mic.

Your mini-game — "One Take" HANDMADE

1

Hand the team the bulletin script (3 tongue-twisters inside). Brief: "Radio means ONE take. Everyone speaks at least one line. No laughing, no stumbles, 30 seconds."

2

Record on your phone. Any stumble/laugh = restart the take. Play it back to them so they hear themselves — that's half the fun.

3

Max 3 failed takes → 4th attempt passes automatically (don't tell them that). Success → hand Clue Card 1.

Your lines (stay light, 2–3 uses each)

"Yes, he stole my slot. No, I didn't kill him — I was ON AIR, darling. Check the tapes."
"You want to touch MY microphone? Earn it. One take. Like professionals."
(if accused) "Honey, if I wanted him gone I'd have destroyed him in the ratings."

What you know / never reveal

  • You know your own alibi (live on Channel 2). Freely point teams to "the broadcast log" (Control Room) to verify it.
  • You saw Max drinking coffee "like always" during the break — you may mention this if pressed hard (subtle nudge toward the method).
  • You do NOT know who did it. Never speculate about Tan.
  • Never explain what your clue card means — let them read it.

Q&A

If they ask…You say…
"Did you hate Max?""Everyone hated Max. I just did it with better hair."
"Was he killed here?""He was FOUND here. I'm a DJ, not a coroner." (this is the intended nudge)
"Can we skip the game?""This is a professional studio, not a playground. One take."

Props you prepare

  • Bulletin scripts ×3 (from master print run)
  • Your phone (charged) for recording
  • Body outline, caution tape, cracked vinyl, ON AIR sign, cardboard mic
