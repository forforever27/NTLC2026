Rules briefed to players (emcee reads these)

  1. Stay with your team. Every room needs your whole team.
  2. One team per room. If occupied, go elsewhere — there is always a free room.
  3. Win the room's challenge to earn its clue card. Each team can clear each room once.
  4. Suspects may be questioned but never forced. They will lie about only one thing: their guilt.
  5. Accusations at the HQ desk from 0:45: who + how + where. Wrong = 15-minute accusation ban, announced to everyone.
  6. First correct accusation wins. Everyone submits a final guess at 2:15 for the remaining placings.
  7. Don't destroy props. Don't hide clue cards from your own team. Have mercy on the room masters.

Standard operating answers (all crew)

  • "Is X the killer?" → "That's for you to work out, detective."
  • Team stuck on your mini-game >10 min → give one nudge hint; >12 min → let them pass with the clue but tell them to move fast.
  • Team missing / idle → radio the floater.
  • Never confirm or deny any part of the solution, even by facial expression.
  • Never let a team photograph another team's clue cards.
  • Never skip the mini-game "because they're nice kids."

Fairness & anti-chaos levers

Hint desk (HQ)

Any team may buy a duplicate of any room's clue card for a 10-minute wait ("the darkroom is developing your film"). Log it on the scoreboard.

Lock-out

Wrong accusation = that team cannot accuse again for 15 minutes (the terminal enforces it). They can still play rooms.

Pacing check

At the 1-hour mark the floater checks each team's cleared-room count. Any team below 2 clears gets shepherded to an empty room.

If a mini-game breaks

Every station page has a no-tech fallback. Worst case: room master quizzes the team with 3 questions about the case so far, then hands the clue.

Emergency contacts / decisions

  • Game-rule disputes → HQ desk decides, emcee announces. No re-litigating.
  • Injury / real emergency → stop game via PA ("ALL STATIONS, DEAD AIR, DEAD AIR"), all crew to main hall.
